Abbott

  • Understanding Analysis, by Stephen Abbott
  • Good intro textbook, covers things at a reasonably slow pace
  • Has a very good selection of exercises
  • Insufficient for furhter exploration, stops work at a single dimension

Rudin

  • Principles of Mathematical Analysis, by Walter Rudin
  • Also known as Baby Rudin, it is a staple in the mathematics community
  • Very hard, very dense. Not reccomended for a beginner, but those with sufficient mathematical maturity will love it
  • Good amount of exercises, but all exercises are very hard

Fitzpatrick

  • Advanced Calculus, by Patrick Fitzpatrick
  • Good intro for beginners that also covers more material than Abbott
  • Exercise selecetion is good
  • Some theorems in the book are weaker than they need to be in order to make the proofs more understandable
