Axler

  • Measure, Integration & Real Analysis, by Sheldon Axler
  • Good introduction to the subject, Axler is very good at motivating some complex topics
  • Excellent selection of exercises
  • Does not go into as much depth as some other books
  • Arguably the biggest pro: It's free at Axler's website!

Rudin

  • Real and Complex Analysis, by Walter Rudin
  • Also known as Papa Rudin, a famous book in the mathematics community
  • Although not dedicated to Measure Theory, the early chapters develop it and provide a nice exposure
  • As with all Rudin books, its extremely hard to fully parse and to do exercises from

Bogachev

  • Measure Theory, by Vladimir Bogachev
  • Technically two separate volumes, covers nearly everything one needs to know about Measure Theory
  • Exercise selecetion is good, over a thousand across the two volumes
  • Requires some mathematical maturity to read, can be dense at times
