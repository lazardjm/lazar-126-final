Fraleigh

  • A First Course in Abstract Algebra, by John B Fraleigh
  • Covers a very wide range of topics, many only cover groups and rings
  • Slightly old, takes some less popular conventions
  • Exercise section can be bloated with repetitive problems

Dummit and Foote

  • Abstract Algebra, by David Dummit and Richard Foote
  • Very good exercises
  • Good examples and motivations for certain topics
  • Covers slightly less than Fraleigh, but is more up to date

Gallian

  • Contemporary Abstract Algebra by Joseph Gallian
  • Very good at engaging the reader, keeps examples going with extended studies throughout chapters
  • Could use more exercises, leaves little area for practicing
  • Even on the newest version, contains many typos that can be confusing to readers
